What Is a Banana Split?

A legendary dessert that's greater than the sum of its parts.

🍌

The Star: A Banana

A perfectly ripe banana, halved lengthwise, forms the iconic boat that holds everything together. It's not just a garnish — it's the foundation.

🍨

Three Scoops of Ice Cream

Traditionally vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry. Three flavors, three personalities, one perfect dessert. The holy trinity of frozen deliciousness.

✨

The Ultimate Toppings

Whipped cream, hot fudge, strawberry sauce, pineapple topping, crushed nuts, and a bright red maraschino cherry on top. Pure joy in a dish.

Building the Perfect Split

Every layer matters. Here's the sacred order.

1

🍌 The Banana Base

Peel and split a ripe banana lengthwise. Place in a long dish. This is your boat — treat it with respect.

2

🍨 Three Glorious Scoops

Nestled between the banana halves: vanilla in the middle, chocolate on one end, strawberry on the other.

3

🍫 The Sauces

Hot fudge over chocolate, strawberry sauce over strawberry, and pineapple topping over vanilla. Perfect pairing, every time.

4

🍦 Whipped Cream Clouds

A generous dollop (or three) of freshly whipped cream on each scoop. Be generous. This is not a time for restraint.

5

🥜 Crushed Nuts

A sprinkle of chopped walnuts or peanuts adds crunch and a subtle savory note that makes everything pop.

6

🍒 The Crown Jewel

Three maraschino cherries, one per scoop. Bright red, impossibly sweet. The final, triumphant flourish.

A Glorious History

Over 120 years of pure deliciousness.

1904

David Evans Strickler, a 23-year-old apprentice pharmacist in Latrobe, Pennsylvania, creates the first banana split for 10 cents. The world is forever changed.

1907

Wilmington, Ohio holds what is believed to be the first Banana Split Festival, solidifying its place in American dessert culture.

1920s–1950s

The golden era of the soda fountain. Banana splits become a staple at every ice cream parlor in America, a symbol of carefree joy.

1972

A world-record banana split stretching 4.55 miles is created in Selinsgrove, Pennsylvania. Ambition meets dessert.

Today

Banana splits are enjoyed worldwide, with infinite creative variations — but the classic original remains undefeated. Long may it reign.
